## Users

Women across all life stages in India, grouped into four primary segments:

- **Adolescents** — managing hormonal changes, PCOS/PCOD, and body image. Often first-time healthcare app users; may be hesitant or embarrassed.
- **Women (reproductive age)** — navigating fertility, pregnancy planning, sexual health, and stress/work-life balance. Often time-poor and seeking efficiency.
- **Post-partum women** — dealing with PPD, lactation issues, sleep disorders, and need for emotional counselling. Emotionally vulnerable; trust and gentleness are critical.
- **Menopausal women** — managing mood swings, hot flashes, weight changes, depression/anxiety, and bone health. Frequently underserved and want to be taken seriously.
- **Elderly women** — set up by a family member or caretaker. Main use case is appointment tracking. Needs maximum simplicity.

Secondary users: caretakers, family members (especially for elderly mode and pregnancy panic-button flows).

The user is often in a private, emotionally loaded moment — a medical consultation, a mental health check, a pregnancy scare. She expects the app to feel like a safe container, not a public bulletin board.

## Product Purpose

Amruni is an Indian women's health platform that integrates telemedicine, mental health support (NIMHANS), fertility and cycle tracking, pregnancy care, yoga, and elderly appointment management into a single trusted space.

Core features:
1. **Telemedicine** — Video/audio/chat consultation, online prescriptions (one-click shareable), follow-up scheduling
2. **NIMHANS Mental Health Integration** — PHQ-9/GAD-7 tools, "I need help" button, counselling with/without anonymous mode, 24/7 call center gateway
3. **Fertility & Homeopathy** — Cycle tracking (Flo-like), hormonal analysis, medicine reminders and delivery tracking
4. **Yoga Integration** — Videos, appointments
5. **Medical Mode** — Infographic-based medical information screen
6. **Pregnancy Mode** — Shareable health info (medicine, appointments, mood) with a trusted person; panic button tied to hospital pre-booking
7. **Elderly Mode** — Caretaker-setup flow, appointment tracking

Success looks like a woman who books her first telemedicine appointment without hesitation, and returns to track her health without dread.

## Brand Personality

Dignified. Calm. Expert.

Amruni speaks like a senior specialist who also listens — not a chatbot, not a wellness influencer, not a pharma portal. The tone is warm but never informal, precise but never cold. The brand's visual identity (deep black, vivid red camellia flowers, gold/amber accents) signals substance and intention, not decoration.

Emotional goal on first open: a woman should feel **safe and understood** — not judged, not overwhelmed, not sold to.

Reference: Narayana Health app — trusted Indian healthcare brand, professional authority grounded in real clinical credibility. Amruni should feel at least as trustworthy, but with a more human, less institutional warmth.

## Anti-references

- **Clinical/hospital white** — sterile, government-form aesthetic. Any UI that feels like an insurance portal or pharmacy chain is wrong.
- **Loud/overwhelming** — too many colors, dense notification-heavy interfaces, anxious visual energy. This app is used by vulnerable people in private moments.
- **Western wellness SaaS** — mint-and-white minimalism, bold geometric sans, startup-cool. Feels foreign to the Indian women this serves.

## Design Principles

1. **Earned trust, not displayed trust** — Authority comes from precision, consistency, and care in every interaction — not badges, stock-photo doctors, or medical iconography overload.
2. **Quiet authority** — The interface leads through structure and restraint. Color, animation, and emphasis are used sparingly so that when something is highlighted, it matters.
3. **Rooted, not decorative** — The brand's Indian identity lives in purpose and metaphor (the camellia, whole-life care), not surface-level pattern overlays or forced cultural motifs.
4. **One voice across every stage** — From a 16-year-old tracking her first cycle to an elderly woman whose daughter set up the app, the voice stays consistent: respectful, warm, unambiguous.
5. **Privacy is design** — Sensitive data (NIMHANS anonymous mode, pregnancy panic buttons, mental health scores) demands UI that signals safety without calling attention to itself. Never expose fragility; contain it gracefully.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG AA minimum**: 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text, keyboard-navigable, screen reader compatible.
- Elderly mode requires larger tap targets (min 48×48px) and simpler layouts, even within WCAG AA.
- Consider future multilingual support (Kannada, Tamil, Hindi) — design layouts must accommodate text expansion without breaking structure.
- Reduced motion: all animations must degrade gracefully (crossfade or instant) for users with vestibular disorders.
